Hi, my name is

ZACK FISCHMANN

I'm an artist, an engineer, and I like to get things done.

When I was only 19, I hitch-hiked from California to Maine in seven days. Six months later, I was supporting myself by busking on streetcorners and singing in bars and coffeeshops. I have since been involved with numerous musical projects, founded a record label, toured across America, performed hundreds of shows, and produced several albums. You can find my latest work here.

After living on the road for several years, I accepted a full scholarship to study at UC Berkeley. At Berkeley, I completed the Center for Entrepreneurship and Technology Concentration in Engineering Leadership, won first place at the Big Ideas @ Berkeley competition, won Fastest Growing Startup at the European Innovation Academy in Tallinn, Estonia, and founded and served as Co-President of the Center for Entrepreneurship and Technology Student Association. I graduated Phi Beta Kappa with Highest Honors and a 4.0 GPA.

In 2014, I wore the generalist hat at Brandcast, a company built by the founders of BandPage and Famo.us, and funded by Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff. In my role, I managed accounts, helped structure a regular sprint release timeline, developed and led UAT, led bug triage, wrote technical and support documentation, acted as a technical liaison during sales meetings, developed product demo and support videos, and helped turn customer and sales feedback into actionable projects.

I'm currently honing my full stack JavaScript skills at Hack Reactor. I take a pragmatic approach to my projects, and am a believer in lean development and design thinking. You can find me writing code at hackathons in the S.F. Bay Area, drinking beer with friends in Oakland, trout fishing in America, and playing banjo in my underwear at home.
